‘Hobgoblin Hike’ at Cedar Creek Park

Spooky Walk for the Community

The annual ‘Hobgoblin Hike’ is just around the corner! This is a one-of-a-kind free event in our area for the spooky season. These haunted walks are sponsored by Westmoreland County Parks and Recreation. The ‘Hobgoblin Hike’ alternates between four regional parks every year. This year, the ‘Hobgoblin Hike’ will be offered only at the following location, Cedar Creek Park on Saturday, October 21, 2023.

Volunteers Needed

Are you looking for a way to volunteer this Halloween season? Volunteers ages 15 and over are needed to help as trail guides or work at scare stations. There will be a volunteer meeting the week before the hike. Limited costumes and props will be available for loan if you wish. Please call (724) 830-3959 if you wish to volunteer your talents – group participation is highly encouraged!

“We encourage group participation, and we can put groups together at scare stations. The more, the better!”
- Lauren Jones, Parks Spokeswoman

Additional Information

The ‘Hobgoblin Hike’ is recommended for ages eight and over. The hike runs continuously from 7:00 p.m. to 9:00 p.m., and they are a half-mile guided outdoor walk through the haunted woods of Cedar Creek Park. Visitors can expect to be led through tunnels and cemeteries, and past more than 100 zombies, ghosts and monsters. Each time you walk through will never be the same. The ‘Hobgoblin Hike’ has a unique haunted scene. This event is a safe family alternative to conventional trick-or-treating. Parents are encouraged to attend with their children. Monetary donations are greatly appreciated! Food and drink will be available for sale at the end of the trail. Cedar Creek Park is located ¼-mile north of I-70 on State Route 51 in Rostraver Township.
